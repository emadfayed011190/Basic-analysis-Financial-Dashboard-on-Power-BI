3. **Data Collection**:
   - Collect relevant data from various sources, ensuring it includes information on sales, transactions, and revenue across different categories.

4. **Data Cleaning and Preparation**:
   - Clean the data to remove duplicates, errors, and inconsistencies. Transform the data into a suitable format for analysis and visualization.

5. **Select Visualization Tools**:
   - Choose appropriate data visualization tools (e.g., Power BI, Tableau) that will best display the data and meet user needs.

6. **Design the Dashboard Layout**:
   - Create a wireframe or mockup of the dashboard layout, organizing sections for key metrics, visualizations, and filters for user interaction.

7. **Implement Visualizations**:
   - Develop visualizations (e.g., charts, tables, graphs) based on the defined metrics and layout, ensuring clarity and ease of interpretation.

8. **Add Interactivity**:

9. **Test the Dashboard**:
   - Conduct thorough testing to ensure that data is accurately represented, visualizations function correctly, and the dashboard is user-friendly.

### KPIs:
1. **Total Revenue**: The overall revenue generated.
2. **Average Revenue**: Average revenue per transaction.
3. **Total Transactions**: The total number of transactions recorded.
4. **Total Revenue by Region**: Revenue breakdown by different geographic regions.
5. **Total Revenue by Quarter**: Revenue performance segmented by each quarter.
6. **Total Revenue by Products and Store**: Revenue generated by different products across stores.

### Chart Requirements:
1. **Total Revenue Overview**:
   - A prominent card displaying **Total Revenue** (e.g., $127.41M).

2. **Revenue by Region**:
   - A pie chart or donut chart showing **Total Revenue by Region** (Asia, U.K., U.S.A.) to visualize the distribution of revenue.

3. **Revenue by Quarter**:
   - A line chart displaying **Total Revenue by Quarter** to illustrate revenue trends over time.

4. **Revenue by Country and Region**:
   - A bar chart showing **Total Revenue and Avg Revenue by Country and Region** to compare performance across different markets.

5. **Revenue by Products and Store**:
   - A horizontal bar chart or stacked bar chart for **Total Revenue by Products and Store** to compare product performance across different stores.

6. **Monthly Revenue Analysis**:
   - A line chart depicting **Total Revenue and Avg Revenue by Month** to analyze trends in revenue over the year.

7. **Top Sales Representatives**:
   - A table displaying sales representatives, their total revenue, and average revenue to highlight top performers.

This comprehensive set of KPIs and chart requirements will enable the dashboard to effectively communicate the business's financial performance and facilitate data-driven decision-making.
